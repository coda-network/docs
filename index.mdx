Coda is the decision layer for digital asset transactions. It helps
regulated financial institutions decide whether to proceed with a digital asset
interaction before funds are credited, signed, or otherwise acted upon.

Digital asset workflows often depend on information spread across wallets,
customer systems, intelligence providers, and operational tools. Coda brings
the relevant context together, applies the policy your institution defines,
and returns one decision: `ALLOW`, `REVIEW`, or `DENY`. Each decision includes
recorded reasons so product and compliance teams can understand what informed
the outcome.

## Built for institutional control

Coda sits between a digital asset workflow and the systems that inform it. Your
institution remains the policy authority. Your existing systems remain the
sources for their data. Your custody, signing, and execution environments remain
responsible for carrying out approved actions.

This approach gives banks, brokerages, asset managers, qualified custodians, and
digital asset infrastructure providers a consistent authorization layer without
transferring assets, keys, or execution control to Coda.
