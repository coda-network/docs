Coda is the decision layer for digital asset transactions. It helps institutions
evaluate a digital asset interaction before funds are credited, signed, or
otherwise acted upon.

Coda brings three inputs into one decision:

- Wallet and transaction context from the interaction in progress
- Intelligence from systems your institution already uses
- Policy defined by your institution

The result is `ALLOW`, `REVIEW`, or `DENY`, with evidence that explains why the
decision was made.

<Note>
  Coda authorizes actions. It is not custody, analytics, a system of record, or
  a replacement for your existing intelligence providers.
</Note>

## How Coda fits into your environment

Your systems remain responsible for execution, custody, records, and source
intelligence. Coda sits before the action and coordinates the authorization
decision.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Receive the interaction context">
    Coda receives the facts your institution needs to evaluate the action.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Orchestrate configured intelligence">
    Coda brings relevant signals from your existing systems into the decision.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply institution-defined policy">
    Coda evaluates the interaction against the policy your institution controls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return an actionable decision">
    Your workflow receives `ALLOW`, `REVIEW`, or `DENY` with decision evidence.
  </Step>
</Steps>
